About Andrew

I am a recent graduate from the University of Southern California with a background in Economics, Data Science, and Artificial Intelligence applications. Here I intend to share my career story as well as those of the projects I've worked on.

Having been born and raised on the island of O'ahu, I like to think that the vast world of data only became known to me after I first began college. After switching majors from Neuroscience and hearing from my best friend that Data Science is essentially a scientific study of human decisions, I was newly introduced to field after field in the data world. Now I see myself drawn to discovering and solving new, practical, data-driven problems and engineering the implementation of artificial intelligence in our everyday businesses.

They say that storytelling is how data should be expressed. But for me, storytelling was where it began. When I started noticing the differences between the places I lived in, the product changes in the video games I enjoyed, and the rises and falls of the music groups I listened to, I was curious about what underlying factors drove those changes. As a result, I now strive to only continue improving my skills in working with data so that those stories can be shared.

Beyond data, I'm deeply connected with my creative side, where I love to play the viola in orchestras and join gigs to record film scores for my friends. I'm a huge cafe hopping enthusiast, so I can be found in the streets of LA trying out a new refresher or matcha. If anything I've mentioned here interests you, feel free to reach out! Let's have a chat.

Alcohol Usage vs GPA Prediction Analysis

This is a comprehensive project in R analyzing survey data to explore the effects of alcohol consumption, social habits, and personal background on academic GPA. Behavioral patterns were uncovered through applied data preprocessing, correlation heatmapping, and K-Means clustering. Random Forest and Lasso regression models were created with cross-validation to identify key academic performance drivers.
